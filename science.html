Physics Concepts & Equations

  • Energy Transformation: When a ligand binds to a receptor, potential energy is converted into kinetic energy or conformational change energy.
  • Kinetic Molecular Theory: Particles must collide with sufficient energy to bind. Temperature increases molecular motion and collision likelihood.
  • Electric Forces (Coulomb's Law): F = k(q1*q2)/r^2 — governs electrostatic interactions between molecules.
  • Radioactive Decay: N(t) = N0(1/2)^(t/T1/2) — measures remaining activity of a tracer over time.
  • Photon Energy: E = hf — relates energy and light frequency in fluorescence detection.

Physics Problem

Question: A fluorescent ligand emits green light at 525 nm. What is the energy of one photon emitted?

Solution:

E = hf = hc/λ
h = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ Js, c = 3.00×10⁸ m/s, λ = 525 nm = 5.25×10⁻⁷ m
E = (6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 3.00×10⁸) / 5.25×10⁻⁷
E ≈ 3.79×10⁻¹⁹ J

Answer: 3.79×10⁻¹⁹ Joules per photon
